DEFINITION

STEM
Vertical, full-length stroke in upright characters. It is the The stem is the main, usually vertical stroke of a letterform.

SERIF(FOOT)
In typography, a serif is the little extra stroke found at the end of main vertical and horizontal strokes of some letterforms.

BOWL
In typography, the curved part of the character that encloses the circular or curved parts (counter) of some letters such as d, b, o, D, and B is the bowl.

DESCENDER
The portion of some lowercase letters, such as g and y, that extends or descends below the baseline is the descender.

EAR
Typically found on the lower case g, an ear is a decorative flourish usually on the upper right side of the bowl. Similar to a serif, the ear can be a distinctive, identifying element of some typefaces.

ASCENDER
In typography, the upward vertical stem on some lowercase letters, such as h and b, that extends above the x-height is the ascender.

TAIL
In typography, the descending, often decorative stroke on the letter Q or the descending, often curved diagonal stroke on K or R is the tail.

STROKE
The main diagonal portion of a letterform such as in N, M, or Y is the stroke. The stroke is secondary to the main stem(s).
